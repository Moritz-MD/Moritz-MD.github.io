Moritz Diener

PhD student researching human-AI delegation, collaboration, and sustainable information systems.

I am a PhD student at the Applied AI in Service Lab, part of the Digital Service Innovation (DSI) research group at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT). My research lies at the intersection of information systems, human-AI interaction, and computer science.

I study how LLM-based agents are reshaping human work, with a particular focus on task delegation and collaboration between humans and AI. More broadly, I am interested in designing human-centred and effective ways for people to interact with multiple AI agents.

I also care about sustainability and the Sustainable Development Goals. Above all, I am driven by curiosity and a passion for learning.

Research

Human-centred interaction with LLM-based agents

My work explores how humans delegate tasks to AI systems, how collaboration patterns change when agents become more capable, and how multi-agent settings can be designed to remain legible, effective, and accountable.

  • Task delegation and collaboration between humans and AI agents
  • Human-AI interaction design for multiple-agent environments
  • Information systems research grounded in practical digital work settings
  • Sustainability-oriented applications of AI and support for the Sustainable Development Goals

Projects

Applying AI methods to circular construction workflows

Within these projects, I research how to apply and develop human-centered AI methods to support circularity efforts in the construction industry, especially through extracting information from documents.

Active through June 2027

Nachhaltige intelligente Sanierungsmassnahmen (NaiS)

Researching AI-supported methods that help sustainability and renovation work become more informed, human-centered, and actionable in practice.

Starting February 2026

Circular Intelligence for Reinforced Concrete Life Extension (ConCIRCLE)

Exploring how AI can support circular intelligence in the construction sector and improve access to information in complex document collections.
